🌳 Why Trees Matter in Farmland Investments

Trees are nature’s best investment partners. They clean the air, increase biodiversity, and even enhance land value. Studies show that green landscapes can increase property prices by up to 20% (World Economic Forum, 2023).
By growing oxygen-producing trees on your farmland, you’re not just improving the environment — you’re boosting your plot’s long-term sustainability and desirability.

🌱 1. Neem (Azadirachta indica)

Why it’s valuable: Neem, known as India’s “village pharmacy,” continuously releases oxygen and filters toxins from the air.
Benefits:
• Acts as a natural mosquito and pest repellent
• Improves soil health through nitrogen fixation
• Excellent shade provider
Best for: Boundary plantations and driveways at Tarini Farms.

🍃 2. Peepal Tree (Ficus religiosa)

Why it’s valuable: The Peepal tree produces oxygen even at night — a rare quality among trees.
Benefits:
• Symbol of peace and longevity
• Excellent for air purification
• Large canopy provides cooling effect
Best for: Meditation corners or wellness zones within your farm plot.

🌴 3. Areca Palm (Dypsis lutescens)

Why it’s valuable: Named one of the top air-purifying plants by NASA’s Clean Air Study, Areca Palm absorbs harmful toxins like xylene and formaldehyde.
Benefits:
• High oxygen release rate
• Adds tropical aesthetics
• Low maintenance
Best for: Garden landscapes and villa entrances.

🌿 4. Banyan Tree (Ficus benghalensis)

Why it’s valuable: The Banyan, India’s national tree, symbolizes strength and longevity. It forms its own mini ecosystem.
Benefits:
• Massive oxygen output
• Strong soil binding and moisture retention
• Shade for livestock and gatherings
Best for: Community centers or shared green zones at GreenSiri Tapovan.

🌾 5. Arjuna Tree (Terminalia arjuna)

Why it’s valuable: Known for its medicinal bark, the Arjuna tree is ideal for soil conservation and eco-friendly farming.
Benefits:
• Prevents soil erosion near water bodies
• Improves groundwater levels
• Used in Ayurvedic wellness products
Best for: Plantation along lakes or rainwater harvesting areas.

🌸 6. Indian Gooseberry (Amla Tree)

Why it’s valuable: Amla trees are oxygen-rich and fruit-bearing, offering both ecological and financial returns.
Benefits:
• Year-round oxygen and fruit yield
• Boosts immunity through high Vitamin C
• Attracts pollinators and birds
Best for: Walking trails and recreational gardens.
