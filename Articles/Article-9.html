Government Schemes Supporting Sustainable Agriculture in India

Sustainable agriculture is pivotal for ensuring food security, environmental conservation, and the economic well-being of farmers. Recognizing this, the Indian government has launched several initiatives aimed at promoting eco-friendly farming practices, enhancing productivity, and supporting farmers’ livelihoods. These schemes are especially relevant for organizations like Greensiri and its initiative, Tarini Farms and Resorts, which are committed to sustainable living and managed farmlands.

🌱 1. National Mission on Natural Farming (NMNF)

Launched in November 2024, the NMNF is a centrally sponsored scheme with an outlay of ₹2,481 crore. It aims to promote natural farming practices that reduce dependency on chemical fertilizers and pesticides, thereby restoring soil fertility and farmland ecosystems. The mission supports training, certification, and market linkage for farmers adopting natural farming methods.
Source

🌾 2. Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana (PKVY)

PKVY focuses on promoting organic farming through a cluster-based approach. It encourages farmers to adopt traditional agricultural practices, providing financial assistance for organic inputs, certification, and marketing. This scheme aligns with Greensiri’s vision of sustainable and chemical-free farming.
Source

💧 3. Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana (PMKSY)

PMKSY aims to enhance water use efficiency through the motto “Per Drop More Crop.” It integrates various irrigation schemes to ensure water availability and promotes micro-irrigation techniques. Efficient water management is crucial for sustainable agriculture, especially in water-scarce regions.
Source

🌾 4. Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY)

PMFBY provides comprehensive crop insurance to farmers against unforeseen events like droughts, floods, and pest attacks. By mitigating financial risks, it encourages farmers to invest in sustainable and innovative farming practices.
Source

☀️ 5. PM-KUSUM (Kisan Urja Suraksha evam Utthaan Mahabhiyan)

This program encourages the use of solar energy in agriculture by offering financial assistance for the installation of grid-connected renewable power plants and solar pumps. The objectives are to lessen carbon footprints, encourage clean energy, and lessen farmers’ reliance on diesel and electricity.
Source

🌿 6. National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture (NMSA)

NMSA focuses on enhancing agricultural productivity in a sustainable manner. It promotes practices like integrated nutrient management, soil health management, and climate-resilient farming techniques. The mission supports capacity building and provides financial assistance for adopting sustainable practices.
Source

🌾 7. Viksit Krishi Sankalp Abhiyan (VKSA)

VKSA is a nationwide campaign launched in May 2025 to bridge the gap between agricultural research and farmers. Agricultural scientists and experts visit farms to provide tailored solutions, promote sustainable practices, and educate farmers on government schemes.
Source

🌿 8. Digital Agriculture Mission

Approved in September 2024 with an outlay of ₹2,817 crore, this mission aims to create digital public infrastructure for agriculture. It supports initiatives like the Digital General Crop Estimation Survey (DGCES) and other IT projects to enhance decision-making and resource optimization in farming.
Press Release: Press Information Bureau

🌾 9. National Mission on Edible Oils – Oilseeds (NMEO-Oilseeds)

The goal of NMEO-Oilseeds, which was introduced in October 2024 with a total investment of ₹10,103 crore, is to increase domestic oilseed production and attain edible oil self-reliance. The goal encourages the use of high-yielding oilseed cultivars and effective water management, among other sustainable measures.
Source

🌱 10. Clean Plant Programme (CPP)

Approved in August 2024 with an outlay of ₹1,765.67 crore, CPP focuses on providing disease-free planting material to enhance the quality and productivity of horticultural crops. It supports the dissemination and adoption of climate-resilient varieties, contributing to sustainable horticulture practices.
Source

🌾 11. National Horticulture Mission (NHM)

NHM aims to promote holistic growth of the horticulture sector through area-based, regionally differentiated strategies. It supports the development of infrastructure, technology dissemination, and capacity building to enhance productivity and sustainability in horticulture.
Source

Conclusion

These extensive programs and initiatives demonstrate the Indian government’s dedication to sustainable agriculture. These programs present worthwhile chances for companies like Greensiri and its project, Tarini Farms and Resorts, to adopt eco-friendly methods, support a sustainable future, and align with national goals.
