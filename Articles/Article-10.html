🌿 Why Agri-Tourism is Thriving in Bangalore

Bangalore, India’s Silicon Valley, is known for its fast-paced tech life and high-rise apartments. However, a quieter movement is growing at the city’s edges — where managed farmlands and agri-tourism merge into a sustainable and high-return investment model.
Agri-tourism blends agriculture, tourism, and education, giving city dwellers a meaningful connection with rural India. The sector is booming — valued at $3.7 billion (IBEF, 2023) — and Bangalore leads this nationwide shift.

Key Drivers of Growth
• Urban Exodus: Around 74% of Bengaluru’s professionals seek weekend getaways within 100 km (Magicbricks, 2023).
• Organic Lifestyle: The city consumes 30% of India’s organic produce, growing at 25% annually (APEDA, 2024).
• Rising Connectivity: Infrastructure like the Bangalore–Mysuru Expressway and STRR enhances access to nearby farmland zones.

Managed farmlands, supported by professional operators like GreenSiri, are perfectly positioned to capitalize on this rising demand.
